Charity As Investment

You probably got here by accident... There's nothing for sale here. This is website should be considered more like the static section of a blog on charity as investment. It's a manifesto on Wisdom Capitalism and Buddhist Economics.

But since your attention is here, feel free to read the important disclaimer below, and leave mindful of at least one suggestion offered by the Buddha on capital accumulation (just in case money making ambitions led you here): don't gamble.

Although this website is riveted with TradingView charts, it isn't a marketing channel. Its purpose is to convey certain messages: It will caution you against gambling [referring you here] and try to encourage you to give charitably to the Theravādin Saṅgha.

Notice

This website is not a solicitation. Mitzvah Capital LLC does not solicit funds for investment or trading.

Mitzvah Capital LLC is a trading operation/vehicle that invests solely for the benefit of its owner. The aspiration is to trade actively and use profits to finance meditation retreats and charitable giving initiatives (specifically "dāna for monastics") if you care to know what Mitzvah Capital does.

May you similarly be inspired to give.

Monastics are cool, however Mitzvah Capital LLC is not affiliated with any monastery or Theravādin Saṅgha.

This is a website presents a Buddhist spin on "mitzvah". So holler if you want to discuss the Buddha's teachings. While no financial or investment advice will be forthcoming, please reach out via social media to discuss the Dhamma anytime.

This website contains tickers, charts, links to charts, and tables of financial instruments and markets. However, none of the information presented on this website should be construed as financial or investment advice.

The Buddha's financial advice was to give generously to ethical people. Even if you are not into Buddhist suttanta, it's common sense to invest in a wholesome (kusala in Pāḷi) mind. In particular, it's great to invest in your own kusala mind. Consequently, kusala consciousnesses and the benefits of charitable giving from a Buddhist perspective are definitely presented. But it's not advice-mongering. Anything presented on this website is strictly for educational purposes. Always do your own research. If you are an aspiring trader/investor, you definitely don't want to be here. Perhaps start your trading voyage and education with an extensive study of the Pāḷi Sutta Piṭaka before proceeding to get a Wharton MBA or CFA qualification. Just saying... And still not offering any kind of financial advice.
