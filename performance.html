Practicing Renunciation

Nekkhamma is a Pāḷi word. It means renunciation and it is another one of ten trancendental qualities (or pāramitās in Pāḷi) of Theravāda Buddhism. Meditation practice should be rooted in the cultivation of this pāramī. In other words Nekkhamma is the practice of "letting go".

Nekkhamma cultivates equanimity. Equanimity means dampening the swings between greed and aversion. Greed and aversion play on our tendencies to like or dislike the conditions we find ourselves in. The destruction of greed and aversion is one way to regard the ultimate goal of Theravāda Buddhism

It is prudent for traders to avoid swinging the pendulum of human nature swinging between likes and dislikes. Doing so allows us to view markets more objectively. Being object, we only concern ourselves with the reality of the situation and "let go" of the emotions attached to that reality.
