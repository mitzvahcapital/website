Practicing Patience

Khanti is a Pāḷi word. It means patience and it is one of ten trancendental qualities (or pāramitās in Pāḷi) of Theravāda Buddhism. Spiritual practice should be rooted in the cultivation of this pāramī.

The pāramī of khanti not only serves to those aspiring to cross the flood to the "far shore" (and thereby achieve the extinguishment discussed here), but also benefits traders and investors. For example, swing traders like Raghee Horner, turn the virtue of patience into profit using watchlists and waiting.

Her mean reversion stock trading method profits from waiting on "exhaustion" and is quite explicit:

  1. Review the relative strength (or weakness) of stocks only after analysing the index and sector they belong to.
  2. Make bullish (price up for the year and above the 200 day SMA) and bearish (price down for the year and below the 200 day SMA) watchlist.
  3. Wait for a trending stock to revert to its 34 period EMA [or an appropriate level of a "daily price movement range"].
  4. Go long (or short) in congruence with the trend.

Be like Raghee.

Don't gamble and trade financial instruments patiently. Of course, give generously. Give gladly. Ideally, give to Theravādin monastics. That's the Mitzvah Capital modus operandi.
